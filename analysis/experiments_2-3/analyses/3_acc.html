Summary

For accuracy, we observed a significant interaction of field by level by handedness, with left handers showing reduced LVF global>local bias ( = 0.68, 95% CI [0.56, 0.82], \(\chi^2(1)\) = 14.62, = .0001, two-sided). Both groups showed LVF global>local bias: the odds ratio of correct/incorrect responses for local/global, RVF/LVF stimuli for right handers was 2.56 (95% CI [2.21, 2.97], = 12.46, < .0001, two-sided); for left handers, 1.73 (95% CI [1.52, 1.97], = 8.17, < .0001, two-sided).

Within the local level, left handers showed significantly reduced right hemifield bias compared to right handers ( = 1.48, 95% CI [1.32, 1.66], = 6.73, < .0001, two-sided). Right handers showed higher accuracy for local stimuli in the RVF than LVF ( = 1.58, 95% CI [1.45, 1.72], z = 10.60, p < .0001, two-sided), and left handers showed marginally higher accuracy for local stimuli in the RVF than LVF ( = 1.07, 95% CI [1.00, 1.16], = 1.73, = .083, two-sided).

Stats

Accuracy is modeled as a binomial effect of field, level, and handedness, using binary correct/incorrect data from every target-present trial:

glmer( correct ~ field*level*handedness + (1 | subject), family = "binomial" )
